📌 Project Overview :- 

AtliQ Motors is an automotive giant from the USA specializing in electric vehicles (EV). In North America, their market share rose to 25% over the last 5 years. As part of their expansion plans, they want to launch their bestselling models in India, where their current market share is less than 2%.

This project provides a detailed market study of the existing EV and Hybrid market in India to help Bruce Haryali (Chief of AtliQ Motors India) make data-driven decisions for a successful launch.

🎯 Business Problem :- 

The goal of this analysis is to answer key strategic questions regarding the Indian EV landscape, including:

Which states are leading in EV adoption?

Who are the top competitors in the 2-wheeler and 4-wheeler segments?

What are the revenue and sales trends projected through 2030?

Where are the untapped "Greenfield" opportunities for market entry?

📊 Key Features of the Report
The project consists of a 3-page interactive Power BI report structured using a clean Star Schema data model:

Executive Summary Page: A high-level view of market size, key performance indicators (KPIs), and overall growth metrics for top executives.

Regional Performance Page: A deep dive into state-wise data comparing total vehicle growth against EV penetration rates.

Market Share Page: An analysis of top manufacturers (like Tata Motors) and their market dominance across different vehicle segments.

💡 Key Insights & Business Value
Growth Modeling: Developed DAX formulas for CAGR and revenue trends, uncovering a 131% growth potential in the 4-wheeler segment by 2030.

Greenfield Opportunities: Identified high-potential untapped markets (such as Meghalaya) where overall vehicle sales are growing rapidly but EV adoption remains very low, offering a first-mover advantage.

Optimized Visual Storytelling: Replaced complex/cluttered charts with high-impact visuals (like Clustered Bar Charts and Funnel Charts) to lower cognitive load and deliver clear data-driven strategies.

🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools Used
Power BI Desktop: For data modeling, dashboard creation, and UI/UX design.

Power Query: Used to validate the high-quality raw datasets and align the date configurations with the Indian fiscal year (April–March).

DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Created 10+ custom measures for tracking CAGR, market share, and revenue metrics.

Excel: For handling raw multi-source CSV datasets.
